---
title: Moofit and Runner Up
date: 2026-01-22
---
So to exercise a bit more "scientifically", I decided to get a heartrate
monitor.  I've had a fitness tracker before and the optical wrist sensor
didn't work well for me, so I wanted to try a chest-strap device.  They 
are quite expensive though.  Anyhow, after quite a bit of searching, I 
found a reasonably cheap one that doesn't have wall-to-wall bad reviews 
and bought it.  The device is called Moofit, and it was claimed that it
interfaces with the Polar apps.  It does, but Polar deliberately limits
functionality for non-Polar devices, so the feature I wanted - heartrate
zone training - doesn't work with the Polar apps.  However, I found an
open source app that does.  Runner Up.  It works fantastically.  The 
voice coaching is a bit "robotic" but I can live with that.  I have 
set it up and taken it for a successful test workout.  Over the next
few weeks I plan to use it for the walk to the station and back each
day and a longer walk on the weekends.  Hopefully it continues to 
impress, otherwise it might be more e-waste and I'll have to splash
out five times as much to get a Polar sensor.  Yes!  They are five
times the price.
